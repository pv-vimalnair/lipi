Liquid Glass — In Context

Full screen vs. Windowed

Two states, same design system, totally different visual impact. The Liquid Glass effect is always present (specular highlight, squircle, gel translucency, soft shadow) but it really comes alive when the app is sitting on top of other content — that's where you see the actual glass.

Full screen

Subtle effect

The app fills the whole viewport. Glass is there — specular shine on top, squircle corners, soft shadows — but the "behind" is just the app's own white background. You feel the glass more than you see it.

Windowed on desktop

Dramatic effect

The app is a floating window on a Windows 11 desktop. Now you see the glass: the desktop wallpaper bleeds through every panel, the squircle bezel refracts the colors around each surface, the float-shadow separates the app from the desktop.

What you see differently in each state

same code · different visual impact
Squircle bezel
Visible in both

The 24px squircle corner is the first thing your eye registers. It's there in full screen too — just less dramatic without something to refract against.

Specular highlight
Visible in both

The bright shine on the top edge of every panel. Pure CSS, works identically. Adds the "wet gel" feel.

Translucent body
Dramatic in windowed

This is the part that needs something behind it. In full screen, you see white-on-white. Windowed, you see the sunset bleeding through.

Float shadow
Dramatic in windowed

The soft drop shadow only "lifts" the app when there's something underneath. In full screen, it's invisible. On a desktop, it sells the depth.

Refraction (bending)
Windowed only · future phase

Real light-bending needs SVG displacement maps or WebGL — Chromium-only. CSS blur is the closest we get today. Refraction is the future phase, not the ship-now one.

OS chrome (Mica)
Windowed only · Tauri setting

Windows 11's native Mica material on the Tauri window frame itself. tauri-plugin-decorum. 5-line add.

Honest takeaway for the design

If you ship Liquid Glass in Lipi, ~80% of the visual impact is already in the CSS — squircle, specular, gel translucency, soft shadow. It works in full screen (subtle but there) and sings in windowed mode (dramatic). For the last 20% (real refraction), you need WebGL — future phase. For OS-native chrome integration, you add tauri-plugin-decorum and get Windows 11 Mica on the window itself — 5 lines of code.

Mock for review. Not committed. Once you sign off, the actual implementation in Lipi is ~150 lines of CSS in tokens.css + 10-12 surface class updates + the Monaco theme flip + tauri-plugin-decorum.
